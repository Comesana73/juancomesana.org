# Books

<section class="book" markdown="1">
  <img src="/assets/images/being-rational-and-being-right.webp" alt="Cover of Being Rational and Being Right">
  <div markdown="1">

My book *Being Rational and Being Right* was published in 2020 by [Oxford University Press](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/being-rational-and-being-right-9780198878711?cc=us&lang=en) (you can also buy it on [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Being-Rational-Right-Juan-Comesaña/dp/0198878710/ref=tmm_pap_swatch_0?_encoding=UTF8&qid=&sr=)). In it, I argue against two prominent views of what our evidence is and for a third one. According to the first view (which I call Psychologism in the book), our evidence is constituted mainly by our experiences. According to the second view (which I call Factualism in the book), our evidence is constituted by what we know. I argue that both of these views misrepresent our evidence, and have untoward consequences for what we are justified in believing and doing. The view I advocate for (Experientialism) has it that our evidence is constituted by what we are basically justified in believing. Along the way I argue that the normative force of unjustified beliefs is best understood in the model of the conditional obligations of dyadic deontic logic, I examine the "easy knowledge" problem and I relate the view developed in the book to Evidentialism, Externalism and hybrids of these views (including my own Evidential Reliabilism).

*Skepticism: The Basics*, written with my father [Manuel Comesaña](https://mdp.academia.edu/ManuelComesa%C3%B1a), was published in April 2022. This textbook has two parts: one which introduces basic concepts of the theory of knowledge (the tripartite conception of knowledge, the Gettier problem, theories of justification, etc.), and a second one which deals with three types of skepticism--skepticism about induction, Cartesian skepticism, and Pyrrhonian skepticism. You can order from [Routledge](http://routledge.pub/Skepticism-The-Basics) or [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Skepticism-Basics-Juan-Comesana/dp/1032077107/ref=sr_1_1?crid=N6HGA4JFKM8X&keywords=skepticism%20the%20basics&qid=1647968705&sprefix=skepticism%20the%20basics%2Caps%2C193&sr=8-1).

*Knowledge and Rationality*, co-edited with [Matthew McGrath](https://sites.wustl.edu/matthewmcgrath/), was published in May 2024. It collects essays by prominent epistemologists in honor of Stewart Cohen. The authors are: Thomas Kelly, David Sosa, Jonathan Vogel, Richard Fumerton, Gideon Rosen, Ángel Pinillos, Zachary Goodsell and John Hawthorne, Shyam Nair, Kate Nolfi, Ernest Sosa, Mona Simion, and Earl Conee and Richard Feldman.
